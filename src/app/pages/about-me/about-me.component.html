My name is Adam, I'm 20 and really into multimedia/developping stuff.

I've been drawing since I'm a kid, mostly stickmen and cartoonish characters. I was so into my universe that I made cartoons of my characters, with a lore and a bunch of adventures.
When I was in school, I was sharing my creations with a few friends who had quite similar interests. One of them made me discovered a site named stickpage.com, and I was stunned seeing a lot of people, like me, liked to share their nerdy universe with animations and games. I would spend hours discovering new animations, new animators and new universes.

Around 2013, I accidently downloaded on the Play Store a node-animating app called Stick Fighter (I was aiming for an actual fighting game üò≠), that I enjoyed and adopted quite quickly. At first I only shared my creations with my friends, but after a few months I decided to put my work on a YouTube channel.
I was awful at animation, but I met a whole lot of people that supported me and liked my universe. This really really helped me improving over the years, making me discover new softwares, techniques and an amazing community.

Around my entrance in college, I had to take a huge break from animating to focus on my studies. Plus, the lack of equipment (graphic tablet, decent computer) was considerably slowing my improvement.
But hopefully, my studies were focused on computer engineering and taught me web designing and software developping.
With my skills in animating and my knowledge in Java, C++, etc. I've crafted on my own a bunch of little games I've always dreamt about.
I'm really passionate about customizing every detail, from game mechanics to sprites, always from scratch.

One of my greatest wishes is that my creations reach as many people as possible. I want my stuff to inspire people like other animators inspired me by the past. If my style and universe can be recognized among other creations, I would consider my goal accomplished. :D
